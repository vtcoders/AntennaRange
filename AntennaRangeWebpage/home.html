Welcome

Welcome to the Antenna Range Project! This project consists of both Virginia Tech undergraduate researchers and faculty from Virginia Tech and Weber State University. The initial goals are to duplicate or re-create the portable antenna range developed by Dr. Christian Hearn and colleagues at Weber State University, to make the range remotely accessible, to increase portability of the range, and to add signal processing to reduce the effect of indirect paths between the source antenna and the antenna under test.

Goals

Current

- Make antenna range controllable and observable by Internet

- Enhance portability and packaging of the range

- Develop digital signal processing to reduce effect of reflections

Long Term

- Initiate a vertically integrated project (VIP), a multi-year, self-sustaining undergraduate project

- Develop a national distributed resource for undergraduate research, education, and outreach

- Increase accessibility of antenna measurements

- Continually enhance and improve the range and antenna measurement capabilities

- Add measurements of installed antennas using drones, etc.
